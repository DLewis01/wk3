A Year of Code - Week 3  

Last week’s Australian budget brought a flood of memes… but not much understanding of the actual economics. So I did something about it.

Introducing my simulation of the MONIAC Hydraulic Computer — yes, a computer powered by WATER.  

A wise lecturer once told me: before you propose anything, get the modelling right. And the gold standard? Keynes. His theories shaped post-war recovery, and dismissing them still isn’t wise. Margaret Thatcher once declared “Keynes is dead” — then pushed monetary policy, sending 1980s England into a deep recession many argue it's still recovering from.

The MONIAC was built on Keynesian economics. And who better to turn the flow of money into the flow of water than Bill Phillips?  

Phillips wasn’t just any economist — he was an inventor, soldier, POW, and later a professor at the London School of Economics. His MONIAC used bottles, valves, pumps, and water to model an economy.

What’s brilliant? It’s tactile. You can see your changes happening in real time — pipes filling, levels shifting — not just invisible bits in a chip. And anyone can build one. A scrap piece of wood, hot glue, aquarium tubes, twist valves, a pump. Everything from Bunnings or your local fish shop.  

But this is my #YearOfCode — so here’s the digital version.
